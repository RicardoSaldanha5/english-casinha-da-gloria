The Fascinating History of Silva

Walk through the Pages of its History

According to historical records Silva was already an established parish in the 12th century. Take a stroll through its picturesque streets and imagine life during the medieval era, when Cistercian monks from the Galician monasteries of Oia or Tominho also walked these same paths. In 1513, Pope Leão X approved a significant exchange, bringing Silva under the jurisdiction of the diocese of Braga. This historical change, marked with great pride, resonates within the community to this day.

The Church of Santa Maria de Silva

Dating back to the 12th century, this church is a true testament to Silva's rich heritage. The church features a checkerboard pattern in silver and red on its heraldry, a symbol of the Cistercian order. Visiting this monument allows you to travel back in time and experience the serenity and spirituality emanating from each ancient stone.

Tradition and Rural Work

Don't miss the opportunity to admire the coat of arms of Silva. It features a black hoe, representing agricultural labor, and a green bramble, a reference to the parish's name. This coat of arms encapsulates the hardworking spirit and deep connection to the land that defines the character of the villagers. Take a stroll through the green fields and experience the peace and beauty of rural life.

Get ready to fall in love with Silva, a historical gem that invites you to discover its history and create lasting memories. We are excited to welcome you to our magical land!
